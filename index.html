Mashiro Sakurai

A figurehead in the gaming development community.

Here is a timeline of Sakurai's life and gaming developments:

  • 1970 - Born August 3rd in Masashimurayama, Tokyo, Japan.
  • 1989 - Begins working at HAL Laboratory to start his videogame development career.
  • 1990 - Creates his first iconic character, Kirby. Begins the direction of his very first videogame title, Kirby's Dreamland.
  • 1992 - Kirby's Dreamland is released for the Nintendo Gameboy in Japan.
  • 1996 - Third title in the Kirby series, Kirby Super Star, is released for the Super Famicon. Development of the plan for an adventure-fighting, then called Ryuoh: The Fighting Game, game is completed.
  • 1997 - Presents the prototype of the previously mentioned fighting game to Nintendo. Full scale development begins at HAL to create a prototype with Nintendo characters for a second presentation.
  • 1999 - Nintendo All Stars! Super Smash Bros. is released for the Nintendo 64. Just a few months after release, Sakurai decides to begin development of a second Smash Bros. game for the Nintendo Gamecube.A plan is created and Sakurai began development of the game on his own.
  • 2001 - Kirby receives an animated television series, Kirby: Right Back At Ya!, that ran for two years in Japan. Super Smash Bros. Melee is also released this year for the Nintendo Gamcube and is refered to as the worst and most grueling development cycle of Sakurai's career.
  • 2002 - Sakurai's first entry for the Gameboy Advance, Kirby: Nightmare in Dreamland, is released in Japan
  • 2003 - Sakurai leaves HAL Laboratory due to feeling that the current organization would be difficult to continue making games with and he desired to work with other creators.
  • 2005 - Sakurai signs on to work on a Mushiking game. Sakurai catches wind of a new development in the Smash Bros. Franchise and decides to join his friend and mentor, Iwata, to create Super Smash Bros. Brawl for the Nintendo Wii. Sakurai and Michiko Takahashi establish their own development company, Sora Ltd.
  • 2006 - The Mushiking game is released in Japan.
  • 2008 - Super Smash Bros. Brawl is released for the Nintendo Wii, marking the third entry in the Smash Franchise. Later this year, Sakurai marries his long time partner, Michiko Takahashi.
  • 2009 - Project Sora starts in Tokyo Idabashi to begin development of Kid Icarus: Uprising.
  • 2011 - The development of the 4th iteration of the Smash Franchise, Super Smash Bros. for the Wii U and 3DS, is announced.
  • 2012 - Kid Icarus: Uprising is released for the Nintendo 3DS and project Sora is closed. The game is well received and the Project Sora website is taken offline.
  • 2014 - Smash Bros. for Wii U and 3DS begin staggering releases throughout the world. The game is well received and releases with news of downloadable content (DLC) coming in the future; a new development for Smash.
  • 2015 - The first DLC is officially announced for Smash Bros for the Wii U and 3DS marking the beginning of a new trend for future Smash Bros games.
  • 2018 - The fifth and final iteration of the Smash Bros. franchise is released. Super Smash Bros. Ultimate for the Nintendo Switch, became the most popular modern fighting game with 15.71 million copies sold. DLC is released with and throughout the life of the game.
  • 2021 - The final DLC character for Super Smash Bros. Ultimate, Sora, is released, marking the end of Sakurai's work on the franchise.
  • 2022 - Sakurai was awarded Best Creator by Weekly Famitsu. Sakurai also announced that he is working on a new project not related to game production, marking a new beginning in his career.

"I don't think "I've ever made something that I'm totally satisfied with. That feeling of doubt, or wanting to do more, is my engine to move forward and make the next iteration."

-- Masahiro Sakurai
